Extended OPTIMUM - Detroit Engine

Extended OPTIMUM engine coverage is available for Detroit DD5®, DD8®, DD13®, DD15®, and DD16® engines that do not have an active manufacturer base warranty or extended coverage.

Extended OPTIMUM begins on the date the coverage is purchased. Time and distance limits are measured from the contract purchase date of the product and start from the current miles/kilometers on the odometer/hubometer.

Coverage ends once the time or mileage limit of the purchase plan is reached.

  • On-Highway units: Coverage is limited to 750,000 miles (1,207,500 km) or 7 years, whichever occurs first.
  • Vocational units: Coverage is limited to 500,000 miles (805,000 km) or 7 years, whichever occurs first.
  • Medium-duty units: Coverage is limited to 400,000 miles (644,000 km) or 7 years, whichever occurs first.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Extended Coverage?

Extended Coverage protects your truck beyond the manufacturer’s base warranty with OEM-backed plans for engines, chassis, and more.

How long does the Extended Coverage last?

Coverage length depends on the plan and vehicle type, typically limited by time or mileage, whichever occurs first.

What package covers the engine aftertreatment?

Enhanced and Ultimate packages include aftertreatment components beyond the Basic engine coverage.

What is the difference between Extended NEXT and Extended OPTIMUM?

Extended NEXT and Extended OPTIMUM are different product families designed for different truck and warranty situations. Enter your VIN to see what you qualify for.

Can I cover multiple trucks in my fleet, including different makes and models?

Yes — coverage eligibility is evaluated per VIN so mixed fleets can be reviewed truck by truck.

Can I purchase Extended OPTIMUM towing with my Extended NEXT chassis coverage?

Standalone options such as towing may be available depending on eligibility. Check eligibility for your VIN to confirm.
